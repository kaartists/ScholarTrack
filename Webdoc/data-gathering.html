Section 01

Consent Form

Informed consent letter presented to each participant prior to the usability evaluation.

Dear Respondent,

Greetings!

We are third-year Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) students from Partido State University. As part of the partial fulfillment of our requirements in Human-Computer Interaction 2 (HCI 2) Final Project, under the guidance of our course instructor, Mr. Michael B. Amata, we are conducting a usability evaluation of our system prototype entitled ScholarTrack.

ScholarTrack is a system designed to simplify scholarship applications, document submissions, and application status verification. This evaluation aims to gather your feedback regarding the system's usability, efficiency, ease of navigation, and overall user-friendliness.

As a participant, you will be asked to interact with the ScholarTrack prototype by exploring selected features such as the Document Vault and Application Status screens. After using the prototype, you will be requested to answer a System Usability Scale (SUS) questionnaire based on your experience.

Your participation will take approximately 10 to 15 minutes and is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw from the evaluation at any time without any penalty or consequence. Please be assured that all information collected will be treated with strict confidentiality. Your identity will not be disclosed in any report, presentation, or project documentation, and the data gathered will be used solely for academic and system improvement purposes.

There are no known serious risks involved in participating in this evaluation. Your honest feedback will greatly help us identify the strengths and areas for improvement of ScholarTrack, allowing us to develop a more effective, accessible, and user-friendly scholarship management tool.

Thank you very much for your time, cooperation, and valuable participation.

Noted by

MICHAEL B. AMATA Course Instructor, Computational Sciences Department

Section 04

Documentation

Supporting evidence and materials gathered during the active validation phase of ScholarTrack.

This section presents the evidence and supporting materials gathered during the system's active validation phase, focusing on the transparency of the data collection process and the integrity of participant recruitment. Due to the high heat index and the shift to online classes, usability testing was conducted virtually, which presented challenges in reaching busy students. Despite these constraints, we successfully reached a segment of the target population and supplemented the data with randomly selected respondents from various courses to ensure a complete evaluation.

In strict adherence to the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173), private messenger conversations have been excluded to protect respondent confidentiality, while the full suite of survey instruments — including the System Usability Scale (SUS) and Heuristic Evaluation Workbooks — remains available to verify the system's usability.
